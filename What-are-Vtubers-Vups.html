What are Vtubers/Vups?

"Vtuber" is short for "Virtual YouTuber", while "Vup" is short for "Virtual Uploader" on Chinese bullet comment video platform Bilibili. "Uploader", which orginates from Japanese "うｐ主／うぷぬし" and is also known as "Up主" in Chinese, means video contributor. Vtuber and Vup both refer to a person who creates and operates a virtual character or avatar in videos, live streams, and other online content. These avatars are often Live 2D or 3D characters that can be controlled in real-time using motion capture technology and preset animations.

Vtubers are typically anime-style characters with their own personalities, backstories, and fanbases. They have gained popularity in recent years, especially in Japan, and have expanded to other countries like China, Korea, and some English-speaking countries as well. They create a variety of content, including gaming streams, music performances, talk shows, and ASMR. Vtubers interact with their audience through live chats, social media, and virtual events.

The virtual aspect of Vtubers allows for a level of anonymity and creativity that traditional content creators may not have. Additionally, Vtubers can create and interact with their audience at anytime 24/7, since their avatars are not bound by physical limitations.
